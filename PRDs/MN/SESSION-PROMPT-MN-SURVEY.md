## What This Is

Client Satisfaction Tracking (MN-SURVEY) is the sixth process in the Mentoring domain. From the Master PRD:

> The process of collecting and recording client feedback throughout and at the conclusion of an engagement.

**Business Value:**
- Gives CBM direct insight into the client experience at defined intervals
- Enables quality improvement and provides evidence of program effectiveness for funders and partners

**Key Capabilities:**
- Survey delivery at defined intervals
- Response recording linked to client and engagement records
- Results available for reporting and trend analysis

## What We Already Know from Prior Process Documents

The following details about MN-SURVEY have been established in other process documents and must be respected:

**From MN-ENGAGE Section 5.4 (Post-Completion Handoffs):**
- Client Satisfaction Tracking (MN-SURVEY) triggers satisfaction surveys based on session counts generated by MN-ENGAGE: after the 2nd session, every 5 sessions thereafter, and at engagement close.

**From MN-ENGAGE Data Collected (Engagement entity):**
- Total Sessions (MN-ENGAGE-DAT-018): system-calculated — MN-SURVEY uses this to determine trigger conditions
- Total Sessions Last 30 Days (MN-ENGAGE-DAT-019): system-calculated
- Last Session Date (MN-ENGAGE-DAT-020): system-calculated

**From MN-CLOSE:**
- MN-CLOSE defines a **Closed Engagement Survey** that is specific to the closure process and is **distinct** from the periodic satisfaction surveys managed by MN-SURVEY. This was an explicit decision made during the MN-CLOSE interview:
  - Decision: "The Closed Engagement Survey is specific to MN-CLOSE and distinct from the periodic satisfaction surveys managed by MN-SURVEY."
  - The Closed Engagement Survey captures feedback on the completed engagement experience
  - MN-SURVEY captures ongoing satisfaction during active engagements
- MN-CLOSE has an open issue: it has not been determined whether survey responses are captured as fields within the CRM or handled by an external survey tool

**From MN-INTAKE:**
- Client Contact email is used for satisfaction survey delivery

**From the old Mentoring Domain PRD (archived, pre-conversion):**
The following was defined in the original domain PRD under a previous process. This content has NOT been validated through the new interview process and should be treated as a starting point for discussion, not as confirmed requirements:
- Survey Response entity with fields: Engagement (relationship), Session (relationship, optional), Survey Trigger (enum: 2nd Session / Every 5 Sessions / Engagement Close), Survey Date/Time, NPS Score (0-10), Did CBM Help You (yes/no), Would Return to See This Mentor (1-5), Mentor Listened and Understood (1-5), How Could CBM Better Meet Needs (rich text)
- Survey responses visible to Client Administrator, Mentor Administrator, and assigned mentors
- No automatic notification sent to mentor — they see responses on next login

**Open Issue from Master PRD (MN-ISS-005):**
- The specific survey tool to be integrated for satisfaction survey delivery has not been selected. This is owned by Tech Admin with a target of "Before go-live."

## What Needs to Be Defined in This Interview

- How does MN-SURVEY relate to MN-CLOSE's Closed Engagement Survey? Are the survey questions the same, different, or overlapping?
- What triggers periodic surveys — is it strictly session count, or are there other triggers?
- Exactly which survey questions are asked (validate or revise the list from the old PRD)
- Who can see survey responses and how are they surfaced (dashboard, reports, inline on records)?
- Are survey responses stored in the CRM as records, or handled by an external survey tool with summary data synced back?
- How does the survey reach the client — email link, in-app, external tool?
- What happens if a client doesn't respond to a survey? Is there a follow-up or is it simply skipped?
- Does the mentor see individual survey responses for their engagements, or only aggregate data?
- Are there any NPS score thresholds that trigger alerts or actions (e.g., low satisfaction flags)?
- What reporting and trend analysis capabilities are needed?
- All data fields this process references (Section 7) and creates/updates (Section 8)
